PCA vs t-SNE vs UMAP  
High-dimensional data visualization based on dimension reduction algorithm seeks distance structure in data.  
PCA tends to save local distance, while T-SNE and UMAP tend to save global distance.  
T-SNE is a nonlinear dimension reduction technology, which is widely used because it can avoid the overcrowding  
of cluster representation and can represent different clusters in overlapping areas. Its limitations include loss of  
large-scale information (inter-cluster relationships), slow computing time, and inability to effectively represent  
very large data sets.  
UMAP (Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection) is a new learning technique for Manifold reduction.  
UMAP is a very effective visualization and scalable dimensionality reduction algorithm. In terms of visual quality,  
UMAP algorithm has a competitive advantage over T-SNE, but it retains more global structure, has superior  
performance, and is more scalable. In addition, UMAP has no computational limitations on embedded dimensions,  
which makes it a common dimension reduction technique for machine learning.  
Features:  
1. In a small data set, t-SNE and UMAP are not very different (dimensional reduction visual analysis of hundreds  
of cells)  
2
. In the big data set, UMAP has obvious advantages (dimensional reduction visualization analysis of more than  
00,000 cells)  
3
Compared with T-SNE, UMAP also provides useful and intuitive properties and retains more global structure,  
especially continuity of cell subsets.
